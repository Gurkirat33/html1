A. R. Rahman

A. R. Rahman, the "Mozart of Madras," is a musical genius known for his innovative compositions. His work spans Indian cinema, international collaborations, and stage productions. Rahman has won two Academy Awards, two Grammy Awards, a BAFTA Award, and a Golden Globe.

         

         

Lata Mangeshkar

Lata Mangeshkar, the "Nightingale of India," was a legendary playback singer whose career spanned over seven decades. She recorded songs in over 36 Indian languages and received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.

Ravi Shankar

Pandit Ravi Shankar was a virtuoso sitar player who introduced Indian classical music to the Western world. He influenced many musicians globally, including The Beatles, and was instrumental in popularizing the use of Indian instruments in Western music.

Kishore Kumar

Kishore Kumar was a versatile artist who excelled as a playback singer, actor, lyricist, composer, producer, director, and screenwriter. His unique voice and style revolutionized Indian film music, leaving an indelible mark on the industry.

Zakir Hussain

Zakir Hussain is a tabla virtuoso, composer, and percussionist known for his contributions to Indian classical music and world music. He has collaborated with numerous international artists and is considered one of the greatest tabla players of all time.

Shreya Ghoshal

Shreya Ghoshal is a prominent playback singer known for her versatility and emotive performances. She has won numerous awards and has sung in over 20 Indian languages. Ghoshal is recognized for her contributions to both classical and contemporary Indian music.
